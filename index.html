Who I Am

I'm a network engineer and AI founder from The Gambia. With a background in routing, switching, and cybersecurity, I applied 14 years of infrastructure thinking to build two live products: GambiaGPT — The Gambia's first national AI platform — and NetWatchX — a production-grade multi-tenant NOC SaaS platform.

My networking background is my superpower in AI, not a limitation. While most AI developers build things that work technically but fail at the infrastructure layer, I understand latency, uptime, bandwidth constraints, and what it means for a user on a 3G connection in Bansang versus fibre in Banjul. That thinking shapes how everything I build works differently.

In building these platforms I applied Python, FastAPI, React, LangChain, FAISS vector databases, RAG pipelines, Streamlit, Supabase, SNMP, GitHub Actions, and more — not in a classroom, but under real pressure, solving real errors, shipping real products.

I also continue to deliver enterprise-grade transmission design and QoS engineering for ZTE Corporation (Worley Project) — bringing the same infrastructure discipline to every layer of what I build.
